NYC Connector

Connecting individuals and resources!


Built by Coding4Impact, NYC Connector is a project which allows users to easily find and locate places near them where they can volunteer at or donate to, such as soup kitchens, food pantries, SNAP centers, senior centers, food scrap drop-off sites, as well as clothing and homeless shelters. Enter an address or zip code and select the appropriate filters, and resources nearby will be displayed on the map. Click on a pinpoint marker to view information about the resource. It's as easy as that!

Coding4Impact is a student-led club at Hunter College High School, with the goal of developing software and web applications that positively impact the world.

Mission


8.5 Million individuals call New York City their home, and a staggering 1.7 million live below the poverty line and rely on government and charitable sources to sustain the livelihoods of themselves and their families. In such a vast city with so many individuals relying on myriad resources, there are many charities operating on minimal budgets with the objective to help the individuals in need.

NYC Connector was built with the mission to empower the thousands of charities in New York City and the tens of thousands of teenagers and adults looking to make an impact to the people who need it most. Built by the team at Coding4Impact, a student-led club at Hunter College High School, NYC Connector aims to integrate the plethora of service opportunities in the city with those looking to make a difference. NYC Connector deviates from the typical service connector in that it creates a platform for connecting fringe, website-less and often contact-less charities to volunteers. For all individuals looking to volunteer in NYC, our self-built software allows for individuals to view specific resource centers nearby their address, as well as giving direct contact information. In our objective to overcome the obstacle of hunger and poverty in our city, we invite you to join our cause and drive impact within our society.
